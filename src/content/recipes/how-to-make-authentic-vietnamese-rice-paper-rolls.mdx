Vietnamese rice paper rolls (Gỏi Cuốn) are fresh, healthy spring rolls made with translucent rice paper wrappers filled with vermicelli noodles, fresh herbs, vegetables, and protein like shrimp or pork. Unlike fried spring rolls, these are served fresh at room temperature with peanut dipping sauce.

![](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Vietnamese-Rice-Paper-Rolls-1536.webp)

## What You'll Need for Vietnamese Rice Paper Rolls

### Essential Ingredients

**Rice Paper & Base:**

- 16-20 rice paper rounds (bánh tráng), 22cm diameter
- 200g rice vermicelli noodles (bún)

**Protein Options (Choose 1-2):**

- 500g large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 300g pork belly or shoulder, thinly sliced
- 200g grilled chicken breast
- 150g firm tofu (vegetarian option)

**Fresh Vegetables & Herbs:**

- 1 large head butter lettuce or green leaf lettuce
- 1 cucumber, julienned into thin strips
- 2 medium carrots, julienned
- 100g bean sprouts (optional)

**Essential Herb Bundle:**

- 1 large bunch fresh cilantro
- 1 large bunch fresh mint
- 1 large bunch Thai basil
- 1 bunch garlic chives or scallions

### For the Peanut Dipping Sauce (Nước Chấm)

- 3 tablespoons hoisin sauce
- 2 tablespoons natural peanut butter
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 teaspoon sriracha or chili garlic sauce
- 2-3 tablespoons warm water
- 2 tablespoons crushed roasted peanuts
- 1 clove garlic, minced

## How to Make Vietnamese Rice Paper Rolls: Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare All Ingredients (Mise en Place)

**Cook the Protein:**

1. **For shrimp:** Bring a pot of salted water to boil. Add shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes until pink and opaque. Immediately transfer to ice water. Once cooled, slice each shrimp in half lengthwise.
2. **For pork:** Boil pork slices in water with a pinch of salt for 15-20 minutes until fully cooked. Let cool, then slice thinly.

**Prepare the Noodles:** Cook rice vermicelli according to package directions (usually 3-4 minutes in boiling water). Drain, rinse with cold water, and toss with a small amount of oil to prevent sticking.

**Prep the Vegetables:**

- Wash and dry all lettuce leaves
- Julienne cucumber and carrots into matchstick-sized pieces
- Wash and dry all fresh herbs, removing thick stems
- Arrange everything on a large platter for easy assembly

### Step 2: Set Up Your Rolling Station

This is the key to success:

1. Fill a large, shallow dish (like a pie plate) with warm water
2. Place a clean, damp kitchen towel on your work surface
3. Arrange all prepared ingredients within arm's reach
4. Have a serving platter ready for finished rolls

![](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Vietnamese-Rice-Pape-p2-1024.webp)

### Step 3: Soften the Rice Paper Properly

**Critical technique:** Dip one rice paper round in warm water for 2-3 seconds only—just until it begins to soften but still feels slightly stiff. It will continue to soften as you work.

Lay the dampened rice paper on your damp towel. It should become pliable within 10-15 seconds.

### Step 4: Fill Your Rice Paper Roll

**Layer from bottom to top (closest to you):**

1. Place 1-2 lettuce leaves in the lower third of the rice paper, leaving 2-3cm from the bottom edge
2. Add a small handful of vermicelli noodles on the lettuce
3. Layer 2-3 pieces of your chosen protein
4. Add 3-4 strips each of cucumber and carrot
5. Place 3-4 sprigs each of mint, cilantro, and Thai basil

**Portion control tip:** Use less filling than you think. Overfilling is the #1 mistake beginners make.

### Step 5: Roll Tightly and Neatly

**The rolling technique:**

1. Fold the bottom edge of the rice paper up and over the filling
2. Fold both left and right sides inward (like a burrito)
3. Continue rolling tightly away from you, keeping the sides tucked in
4. For shrimp rolls: Before completing the final roll, place 2-3 shrimp halves (pink side down) on the wrapper, then finish rolling so the shrimp shows through the translucent paper

**Pro tip:** Keep constant, gentle pressure while rolling to ensure a tight, neat cylinder.

### Step 6: Make the Peanut Dipping Sauce

1. In a small bowl, whisk together hoisin sauce and peanut butter
2. Add rice vinegar and sriracha
3. Gradually add warm water until you reach a pourable but not watery consistency
4. Stir in minced garlic
5. Transfer to serving bowls and top with crushed peanuts

## Common Questions About Vietnamese Rice Paper Rolls

### How do you keep rice paper rolls from sticking together?

Arrange finished rolls on a platter lined with lettuce leaves, ensuring they don't touch each other. Cover with a damp paper towel and plastic wrap if storing. They can sit at room temperature for 1-2 hours or refrigerate for up to 4 hours.

### Why do my rice paper rolls tear?

The three most common causes are: (1) soaking the rice paper too long, making it too fragile; (2) overfilling the rolls; (3) using rice paper that's old or stored in humidity. The wrapper should feel slightly firm when you start rolling—it will continue to soften.

### Can you make rice paper rolls ahead of time?

Yes, but with precautions. Make them 2-4 hours before serving and store properly covered in the refrigerator. Don't make them more than 6 hours ahead, as the rice paper becomes tough and chewy. They're best served within 2 hours of making.

### What's the difference between rice paper rolls and spring rolls?

Vietnamese rice paper rolls (Gỏi Cuốn) are **fresh** and **uncooked** (except the protein), served at room temperature. Spring rolls are typically **fried**. In Vietnam, fried spring rolls are called chả giò in the South or nem rán in the North.

### Do you eat Vietnamese rice paper rolls hot or cold?

Authentic Vietnamese rice paper rolls are served **at room temperature** or slightly chilled, never hot. The fresh herbs and vegetables are meant to be crisp and cool, providing a refreshing contrast to the warm dipping sauce.

### What can I substitute if I can't find Thai basil?

Regular sweet basil works, though the flavor is different. Thai basil has a distinctive anise-like flavor. Alternatives include: adding a few extra mint leaves, using perilla leaves (if available at Asian markets), or simply increasing the cilantro and mint proportions.

## Expert Tips for Perfect Rice Paper Rolls (From 15+ Years Making Them)

**Temperature matters:** Use warm (not hot) water for soaking rice paper. Cold water takes too long; hot water makes the paper tear easily.

**The two-second rule:** Don't oversoak. The rice paper should still feel slightly firm when you remove it from water. I learned this from my Vietnamese neighbor who makes these weekly for her family.

**Assembly line method:** When making rolls for a group, prepare all ingredients first, then roll all at once. This keeps them fresh and saves time.

**The herb ratio:** Authentic Vietnamese rolls are generous with herbs—they should be approximately 40% of the filling. Don't skimp on the mint and cilantro.

**Rice paper brand matters:** Look for Three Ladies Brand (Ba Co Gai) or Red Rose Brand at Asian markets. These have the best texture and translucency. Check the ingredient list—it should only contain rice flour, water, and salt.

**Keep practice rolls:** Your first few rolls might be messy. That's normal. By the fourth or fifth roll, you'll develop the feel for it. The "ugly" rolls still taste delicious.

## Serving Suggestions

**Traditional presentation:** Arrange rolls on a platter with lettuce leaves between layers. Serve with peanut sauce in individual small bowls. Add lime wedges and extra herbs on the side.

**Make it a meal:** Pair with Vietnamese iced coffee (cà phê sữa đá) and a light soup like canh chua (sweet and sour soup).

**Party-friendly:** Set up a DIY rice paper roll station. Guests love assembling their own rolls with their preferred fillings.

## Storage and Meal Prep

**Short-term storage (2-4 hours):** Arrange rolls on a plate, cover with damp paper towels, then wrap tightly with plastic wrap. Refrigerate.

**What NOT to do:**

- Don't freeze rice paper rolls—the texture becomes rubbery
- Don't stack rolls directly on top of each other
- Don't store with the dipping sauce already applied

**Make-ahead strategy:** Prepare all ingredients the night before. Store proteins, noodles, and vegetables in separate airtight containers. Assemble rolls the day of serving.

## Variations to Try

**Fully vegetarian:** Use marinated tofu, avocado, and extra vegetables.
