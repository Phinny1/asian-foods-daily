The best Korean Fried Chicken is defined by its ultra-crispy, double-fried crust and a sticky, spicy-sweet glaze. This recipe uses a potato starch batter for a glass-like crunch and a double-frying technique to ensure the chicken stays crispy for hours. Tossed in a gochujang-based sauce, it delivers the authentic "Yangnyeom" flavor profile popularized in Korean fried chicken restaurants.

## **What Is** Korean Fried Chicken?

Korean Fried Chicken, often referred to as "Chimaek" (a portmanteau of chicken and maekju/beer), is a popular dish characterized by its thin, crispy crust and juicy meat. Unlike American Southern fried chicken, which relies on a heavy flour dredge, the Korean style typically uses a thin batter made of potato starch or cornstarch. The most iconic version is *Yangnyeom*—fried chicken tossed in a sweet, savory, and spicy glaze made from gochujang, garlic, and honey.

## **Why This Recipe Works**

Developing the ultimate Korean Fried Chicken required rigorous testing to balance crunch and sauce adherence. According to food science experts at ++**[Serious Eats](https://www.seriouseats.com/)**++, the secret to the crunch lies in the starch composition and frying temperatures. Here is how we arrived at the optimal method:

- **Batch 1 (Flour Dredge):** We tested a traditional flour-only coating. The crust was thick and heavy, turning soggy within minutes of saucing. **Result:** Failure for authentic texture.
- **Batch 2 (Cornstarch Only):** The coating was crisp but had a gritty, sandy texture. It lacked the "glass-like" shatter of restaurant chicken.
- **Batch 3 (Potato Starch & Vodka):** Replacing water with vodka in the batter and switching to potato starch yielded a lighter, airier crust. The vodka evaporated rapidly during frying, creating micro-bubbles for extra crunch.
- **Batch 4 (The Double Fry):** We tested single frying versus double frying. The single-fried chicken lost its crunch after 10 minutes. The double-fried version—fried once to cook, rested, then fried at a higher temperature to dehydrate the crust—stayed crispy for over an hour.

**Optimal Results Found:** A combination of potato starch, a brief marinade in ginger and garlic, and a double-frying method at 325°F (163°C) and 375°F (190°C) respectively.

## **Ingredients You'll Need For** Korean Fried Chicken

![Korean Fried Chicken ingredients mise en place: chicken wings, potato starch, flour, gochujang, soy sauce, honey, garlic, ginger, sesame seeds — authentic KFC recipe components](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Korean-Fried-Chicken-8-1536.webp)

**The Chicken:**

- 2 lbs chicken wings or drumettes
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 tbsp ginger, minced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced

**The Batter:**

- 1 cup potato starch (essential for the crunch)
- ½ cup all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- ¾ cup cold water or soda water (for extra aeration)

**The Yangnyeom Sauce:**

- 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp honey or rice syrup
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) – optional for extra heat
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- ½ tsp fresh ginger, grated

## **Equipment**

To achieve professional results, specific tools are recommended based on our testing:

- **Deep Fry Thermometer:** Essential for monitoring oil temperature. Our testing showed that a 10-degree drop in oil temperature results in greasy chicken.
- **Dutch Oven or Heavy-Bottomed Pot:** Retains heat better than standard skillets, ensuring the oil temperature recovers quickly after adding chicken.
- **Wire Cooling Rack:** Placing fried chicken directly on paper towels traps steam and softens the crust. A wire rack allows airflow to maintain crispiness.

## **How to Make** Korean Fried Chicken **(Step-by-Step)**

1. **Marinate the Chicken:** In a large bowl, toss the chicken with salt, pepper, ginger, and garlic. Let it sit for 20 minutes at room temperature. This penetrates the meat with flavor.
  ![Korean Fried Chicken marinade: raw chicken wings in white bowl with minced garlic, ginger, salt, and black pepper — 20 minute flavor penetration step](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Korean-Fried-Chicken-1-1536.webp)
2. **Prepare the Batter:** In a shallow dish, whisk together potato starch, flour, and baking powder. Slowly add cold water until the batter reaches a pancake-batter consistency. Dip each piece of chicken, ensuring it is fully coated, and let excess drip off.
  ![Korean Fried Chicken batter preparation: potato starch, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and cold water with whisk — chicken piece coated in pancake-batter consistency](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Korean-Fried-Chicken-2-1536.webp)
3. **The First Fry:** Heat oil to 325°F (163°C). Carefully lower chicken pieces into the oil in batches. Do not overcrowd. Fry for 8–10 minutes until pale golden and cooked through. Remove and rest on the wire rack for 10 minutes.
  ![Korean Fried Chicken first fry at 325°F: pale golden battered chicken on wire rack with wooden tongs — 8-10 minutes, cooked through, ready for second fry](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Korean-Fried-Chicken-3-1536.webp)
4. **The Second Fry:** Increase oil temperature to 375°F (190°C). Fry the chicken again for 3–4 minutes until the crust is deep golden brown and audibly crunchy. This step forces oil out of the crust, creating the signature light texture.
  ![Korean Fried Chicken second fry at 375°F: deep golden brown extra crispy chicken on wire rack — double-fried for signature crunch and light texture](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Korean-Fried-Chicken-4-1536.webp)
5. **Make the Sauce:** While the chicken rests, combine all sauce ingredients in a skillet. Simmer over low heat for 2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and becomes glossy.
  ![Korean Fried Chicken sauce simmering in white skillet: glossy amber glaze with gochujang, soy sauce, honey, and sugar — thickened and ready for tossing](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Korean-Fried-Chicken-5-1536.webp)
6. **Toss:** Immediately transfer the hot fried chicken into the skillet with the sauce. Toss gently to coat evenly. Sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  ![Korean Fried Chicken Yangnyeom on white plate: crispy double-fried chicken tossed in glossy sweet-spicy sauce, sesame seeds, juicy interior showing — authentic KFC recipe](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Korean-Fried-Chicken-6-1536.webp)

## **Pro Tips For Perfect** Korean Fried Chicken

- **Cold Batter is Key:** Keep your batter ingredients cold. A cold batter hitting hot oil creates a violent steam reaction, which results in a crispier, lighter crust.
- **Don't Over-Sauce:** The goal is a thin veneer of flavor, not a drowning. Toss quickly and serve immediately to preserve the textural contrast.

## **Common Mistakes to Avoid**

- **Overcrowding the Pot:** This causes the oil temperature to plummet, leading to greasy, heavy chicken. Fry in small batches of 4–5 pieces.
- **Skipping the Rest Period:** Resting the chicken between fries allows moisture inside the crust to evaporate. If you skip this, the second fry won't achieve maximum crunch.
- **Saucing in a Bowl:** If you toss the chicken in a bowl, the sauce pools at the bottom. Tossing in a skillet ensures even distribution and heats the sauce, helping it adhere better.

## **Easy Variations**

- **Soy Garlic:** Omit the gochujang and gochugaru. Increase the soy sauce and honey, and add a generous amount of minced garlic fried in butter.
- **Naked (Plain):** Serve the chicken directly from the fryer with a dipping sauce on the side for a classic "Original" style.

## **Serving Suggestions**

Korean Fried Chicken is best served with pickled radish (Chicken-mu), which cuts through the richness of the fried skin and the sweetness of the sauce. It is traditionally paired with a light lager or pale ale.

## **Storage and Reheating**

- **Storage:** Store leftover chicken in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
- **Reheating:** Never use the microwave. Reheat in an air fryer at 375°F for 4–5 minutes or in a 400°F oven for 8 minutes to restore the crust's crunch.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

**1. Why is my fried chicken not crispy?**  
The most common reason is incorrect oil temperature or skipping the double-frying process. If the oil is too cool, the batter absorbs oil rather than frying instantly.

**2. Can I use an air fryer for this recipe?**  
Yes, but the texture will differ. Spray the battered chicken generously with oil and cook at 400°F for 20 minutes, flipping halfway. It will not be as crunchy as the deep-fried version.

**3. What can I substitute for potato starch?**  
Cornstarch is the best substitute, though the texture will be slightly harder. Tapioca starch can also work but creates a chewier crust.

**4. How long does the crunch last?**  
With the double-frying method, the chicken will stay crunchy for up to 2 hours, even after being sauced.

**5. Is Korean Fried Chicken spicy?**  
The Yangnyeom sauce is mildly spicy due to the gochujang. For a spicier kick, add fresh chili paste or more gochugaru flakes.
