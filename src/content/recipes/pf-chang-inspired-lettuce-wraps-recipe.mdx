## **Ingredients You’ll Need**

![A wooden cutting board holds ground meat, chopped mushrooms, a head of lettuce, sliced water chestnuts, chopped green onions, minced garlic, ginger, and three small bowls of sauces—perfect for assembling PF Chang-Inspired Lettuce Wraps.](https://spcdn.shortpixel.ai/spio/ret_img,q_cdnize,to_auto,s_webp:avif/asianfoodsdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PF-Chang-Inspired-Lettuce-Wraps-4-1024x572.png)

Here’s everything for this best PF [Chang](https://asianfoodsdaily.com/this-15-minute-beef-chow-fun-will-change-your-life/) lettuce wraps copycat:

### **For the Chicken Filling**

- **1 pound ground chicken** – The star. Ground turkey works too if that’s what you’ve got.
- **1 tablespoon sesame oil** – Gives that [authentic Asian](https://asianfoodsdaily.com/how-to-make-authentic-asian-beef-stew/) flavor. Don’t skip this.
- **2 tablespoons vegetable oil** – For cooking. Neutral flavor lets everything else shine.
- **3 cloves garlic, minced** – Fresh is non-negotiable here.
- **1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced** – Adds brightness. Jarred ginger works in a pinch.
- **1 can (8 oz) water chestnuts, drained and diced** – That signature crunch.
- **4 green onions, chopped** – Reserve some green parts for topping.

### **For the Sauce**

- **3 tablespoons soy sauce** – Or tamari for gluten-free.
- **1 tablespoon hoisin sauce** – Sweet and savory magic.
- **1 tablespoon rice wine vinegar** – Cuts through the richness.
- **2 teaspoons sriracha** – Adjust to your heat preference.
- **1 teaspoon sugar** – Balances everything out.

### **For Serving**

- **1 head butter lettuce** – Bibb lettuce works great too. You want cups, not flat leaves.

## **How I Make PF Chang-Inspired Lettuce Wraps**

![PF Chang-Inspired Lettuce Wraps feature ground meat stir-fried with diced mushrooms, sliced water chestnuts, and chopped green onions in a wok for a flavorful appetizer or light meal.](https://spcdn.shortpixel.ai/spio/ret_img,q_cdnize,to_auto,s_webp:avif/asianfoodsdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/PF-Chang-Inspired-Lettuce-Wraps-3-1024x572.png)

Here’s my tried-and-true method for these Asian chicken lettuce wraps. Trust me on the order of operations here.

### **Step 1: Make the Sauce First**

Whisk together soy sauce, hoisin, rice wine vinegar, sriracha, and sugar in a small bowl. Set it aside. This takes 30 seconds and means you won’t be scrambling later.

### **Step 2: Prep Your Lettuce**

Carefully separate the lettuce leaves. Rinse them. Pat dry. Cold lettuce is key – it stays crisp against the hot filling. I stick mine in the fridge while I cook.

### **Step 3: Cook the Chicken**

Heat vegetable oil in your skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground chicken. Break it up with your spoon. Cook until it’s no longer pink, about 5-6 minutes. Don’t

move it around too much at first – you want some browning.

### **Step 4: Add the Aromatics**

Push the chicken to one side. Add sesame oil to the empty space. Toss in garlic and ginger. Let them sizzle for about 30 seconds. Your kitchen is going to smell incredible right now. Mix everything together.

### **Step 5: Add Water Chestnuts and Sauce**

Stir in the water chestnuts. Pour the sauce over everything. Add most of the green onions (save some for garnish). Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the sauce coats everything and reduces slightly.

### **Step 6: Serve**

Spoon the filling into lettuce cups. Top with remaining green onions. Serve immediately while the filling is hot and the lettuce is cold.\

## **Tips That Make a Difference**

After making this quick PF Chang copycat recipe about a hundred times, here’s what I’ve learned:

- **Don’t overcrowd the pan.** If your skillet isn’t big enough, cook the chicken in batches. Crowding leads to steaming instead of browning.
- **Fresh ginger matters.** Powdered just isn’t the same. It’s worth the extra minute to mince fresh.
- **Get your lettuce cold.** Like, really cold. The temperature contrast is half the appeal.
- **Taste before serving.** Need more heat? Add sriracha. Too salty? A squeeze of lime helps.
- **Dice the water chestnuts small.** You want crunch in every bite, not big chunks.

## **Storage and Reheating**

Good news: the filling keeps well. Bad news: the lettuce doesn’t.

**Storing:** Keep the chicken filling in an airtight container in the fridge. It’s good for 3-4 days. Store lettuce separately – wrap it in paper towels inside a plastic bag.

**Reheating:** Warm the filling in a skillet over medium heat. Add a splash of water if it seems dry. Microwave works but you’ll lose some texture. Takes about 2-3 minutes either way.

**Meal prep tip:** Make a double batch of the filling on Sunday. Use it throughout the week in lettuce wraps, rice bowls, or even tacos.

## **FAQs About PF Chang-Inspired Lettuce Wraps**

### **What makes PF Chang’s lettuce wraps so good?**

The magic is in the balance. Sweet hoisin, savory soy sauce, the heat from sriracha, and that crunch from water chestnuts. Plus the contrast between hot filling and cold lettuce. This copycat PF Chang’s chicken lettuce wraps nails all of that.

### **Can I make these lettuce wraps ahead of time?**

Absolutely. Make the filling up to 3 days ahead. Prep the lettuce a few hours before serving. Just don’t combine them until you’re ready to eat.

### **How do I make this a gluten-free lettuce wraps recipe?**

Use tamari instead of soy sauce and check your hoisin sauce label. Some brands contain gluten, some don’t. Everything else is naturally gluten-free.

### **What can I use instead of butter lettuce?**

Bibb lettuce is the closest substitute. Iceberg works but it’s less flavorful. Romaine hearts can work in a pinch – use the inner leaves. Some people use cabbage cups or even endive for smaller bites.

### **How many calories are in these healthy chicken lettuce wraps?**

About 180-200 calories per serving (2-3 lettuce cups with filling). It’s a lighter option compared to most takeout. Using lean ground chicken and skipping the rice keeps it reasonable.
