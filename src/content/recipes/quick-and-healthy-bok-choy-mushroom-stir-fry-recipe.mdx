## **Ingredients You’ll Need**

Here’s the lineup—simple, fresh, and easy to find:

- 1 pound bok choy, chopped (stems and leaves separated for texture)
- 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced (button, shiitake, or whatever you fancy)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced (because garlic makes everything better)
- 1 tablespoon ginger, grated (fresh is best for that zing)
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce (low-sodium if you’re watching salt)
- 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional, but it adds depth)
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil (for that nutty finish)
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (or any high-heat oil)
- Salt and pepper to taste

No exotic pantry items here—just stuff you probably already have or can grab on a quick grocery run.

## **How to Make It: Step-by-Step Instructions**

Cooking this stir-fry is like a little kitchen dance—fast, fun, and oh-so-rewarding. Here’s how it goes:

1. **Heat things up**: Pour the vegetable oil into a large wok or skillet and crank it to medium-high heat. You’ll know it’s ready when it shimmers.
2. **Aromatics first**: Toss in the garlic and ginger. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until your kitchen smells like heaven. Don’t let it burn—keep it moving!
3. **Mushroom magic**: Add the sliced mushrooms and cook for 3-4 minutes. They’ll turn golden and release that earthy aroma. Patience here pays off.
4. **Bok choy time**: Throw in the bok choy stems first (they need a bit more time), then the leaves after a minute. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the stems are tender-crisp and the leaves wilt just right.
5. **Sauce it up**: Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and sesame oil. Stir everything to coat—it’s all about even flavor distribution.
6. **Season and serve**: Add a pinch of salt and pepper to taste. Serve it hot over rice, noodles, or whatever you’re craving.

Pro tip: Stir-fry with confidence—hesitation is the enemy of that perfect crisp-tender bite.

## **Tips and Variations to Make It Your Own**

This recipe is a blank canvas begging for your creativity. Here are some ideas to mix it up:

- **Add protein**: Toss in tofu, chicken, shrimp, or beef for a heartier dish. Cook it first, then set it aside and add back in at the end.
- **Veggie boost**: Bell peppers, snap peas, or carrots bring color and crunch. Just adjust the cooking time so nothing gets mushy.
- **Spice it up**: Love heat? A sprinkle of chili flakes or a squirt of sriracha will wake up your taste buds.
- **Mushroom medley**: Try oyster or enoki mushrooms for a different texture. It’s like a fungi party in your wok!
