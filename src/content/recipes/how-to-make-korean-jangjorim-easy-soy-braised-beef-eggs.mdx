## **What Is Jangjorim, Anyway?**

Jangjorim is a Korean classic where beef—usually brisket or shank—gets slow-cooked in a rich broth of soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and a touch of sugar. The result? Meat so tender it practically melts, paired with hard-boiled eggs that soak up all that savory goodness. Sometimes, chili peppers join the party for a little kick.

This dish often pops up as a banchan (side dish) in Korean meals, but don’t let that fool you—it’s hearty enough to star as the main event alongside a bowl of rice. Plus, it’s a chameleon: serve it hot, cold, or straight from the fridge, and it still delivers. Perfect for a cozy dinner or a make-ahead lunch, Jangjorim is the gift that keeps on giving.

## **How to Whip Up Jangjorim at Home**

Ready to bring Jangjorim to your kitchen? This recipe is beginner-friendly and packed with flavor. Here’s what you’ll need and how to make it happen:

### **Ingredients**

- 1 lb beef brisket or shank, cut into chunks
- 4-5 hard-boiled eggs, peeled
- 1 cup soy sauce
- 1/2 cup water
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1-inch piece of ginger, sliced
- 2-3 green onions, chopped
- 1-2 chili peppers (optional, for spice lovers)
- Sesame seeds and sliced green onions for garnish

### **Instructions**

1. **Prep the Beef:** Boil the beef chunks in water for 10 minutes to clean off impurities. Drain and rinse with cold water.
2. **Mix the Sauce:** In a pot, combine soy sauce, water, sugar, garlic, ginger, and green onions. Heat until it simmers.
3. **Cook the Beef:** Add the beef to the pot, making sure it’s covered by the sauce. Simmer on low, covered, for 1.5 to 2 hours until tender.
4. **Add Eggs:** Toss in the eggs and peppers (if using) and simmer for another 10-15 minutes.
5. **Finish Up:** Let it cool slightly, sprinkle with sesame seeds and green onions, and serve with rice.

**Quick Tip:** Patience pays off—Jangjorim tastes even better after a night in the fridge. The flavors get cozy and deepen overnight.

## **Jangjorim FAQs**

Got questions? I’ve got answers:

- **Can I swap the meat?** Sure! Pork, chicken, or even tofu work—just tweak the cooking time.
- **Is it spicy?** Only if you want it to be. Skip the peppers for mild, or pile them on for heat.
- **Slow cooker option?** Yup! After boiling the beef, cook on low for 6-8 hours, adding eggs in the last hour.
- **Gluten-free?** Use tamari or gluten-free soy sauce, and you’re good to go.
