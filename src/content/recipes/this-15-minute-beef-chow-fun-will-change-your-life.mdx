Beef Chow Fun is a classic Cantonese stir-fry featuring tender beef, wide rice noodles (Ho Fun), bean sprouts, and scallions. The dish is defined by *Wok Hei*—the "breath of the wok"—achieved through high-temperature searing and rapid tossing, resulting in a smoky, caramelized finish without excess oil.

## What Is Beef Chow Fun?

Beef Chow Fun, or *Gon Chow Ngau Ho*, is a cornerstone of Cantonese dim sum and street food. Unlike "wet" stir-fries that utilize a thickened gravy, this is a "dry" stir-fry. The goal is to coat every strand of the wide, flat rice noodles in a savory soy-based sauce while maintaining a distinct charred aroma and preventing the noodles from breaking or clumping.

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Velvetting Technique:** Marinating the beef with cornstarch and oil ensures the meat remains tender and succulent despite the intense wok heat.
- **Balanced Soy Profile:** The combination of light soy sauce (for salt) and dark soy sauce (for color and sweetness) creates the iconic deep mahogany hue.
- **High-Heat Control:** By sequencing the ingredients, we maximize the Maillard reaction on the beef and noodles while preserving the crunch of the vegetables.

## Ingredients You’ll Need For Beef Chow Fun

![On a wooden surface, six bowls (arranged in two rows of three) display ingredients for a recipe (likely a stir - fried noodle dish like beef chow fun), each with a label](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Beef-Chow-Fun-3-1536.webp)

**The Protein:**

- **Beef:** 8 oz Flank steak or skirt steak, sliced thinly against the grain.
- **Marinade:** 1 tsp light soy sauce, 1 tsp Shaoxing wine, ½ tsp cornstarch, ½ tsp toasted sesame oil, and a pinch of white pepper.

**The Noodles & Aromatics:**

- **Ho Fun:** 1 lb fresh wide rice noodles (if refrigerated, microwave briefly to soften before separating).
- **Vegetables:** 2 cups fresh mung bean sprouts, 3-4 stalks scallions (cut into 2-inch lengths), ½ yellow onion (sliced).
- **Sauce Mix:** 1 tbsp light soy sauce, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce, 1 tsp sugar, 1 tsp oyster sauce.

### Equipment

- **Carbon Steel Wok:** Essential for achieving *Wok Hei*.
- **Wok Spatula (Chuan):** Designed to get under the noodles without breaking them.
- **High-Output Burner:** To maintain the necessary surface temperature.

## How to Make Beef Chow Fun (Step-by-Step)

![Seared beef strips in a hot pan with oil, showing a golden - brown, juicy exterior (ideal for stir - fries, steaks, or savory recipes). Close - up of tender beef slices cooking, perfect for meal prep, gourmet cooking, or quick dinner idea](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Beef-Chow-Fun-2-1536.webp)

1. **Prep the Beef:** Marinate the sliced beef for at least 20 minutes.
2. **Sear the Beef:** Heat the wok until smoking. Add oil and sear the beef in a single layer until 80% cooked. Remove and set aside.
3. **Aromatics:** Clean the wok, add fresh oil, and stir-fry the onions until translucent.
4. **The Noodles:** Add the rice noodles. Spread them out and let them sear undisturbed for 30 seconds to develop a crust.
5. **Sauce Application:** Pour the sauce mixture around the perimeter of the wok so it sizzles before hitting the noodles. Toss gently using a folding motion.
6. **The Finish:** Return the beef to the wok. Add bean sprouts and scallions. Toss on maximum heat for 1-2 minutes until the sprouts are slightly wilted but still crunchy.
7. **Serve:** Plate immediately to preserve the smoky aroma.

## Pro Tips For Perfecting Beef Chow Fun

- **Noodle Temperature:** Never use cold rice noodles directly from the fridge; they will snap. Bring them to room temperature or steam them slightly first.
- **The "Dry" Rule:** Ensure your bean sprouts are bone-dry before adding them to prevent the dish from becoming soggy.
- **Oil Management:** Use a high-smoke point oil like peanut or grapeseed oil.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Overcrowding the Wok:** This lowers the temperature, leading to "stewing" rather than frying. Cook in batches if using a standard home range.
- **Over-stirring:** Constant stirring breaks the delicate fresh noodles. Use a "lifting and folding" motion.
- **Skipping Dark Soy:** You will miss the depth of color and the subtle molasses-like undertone that defines the dish.

## Easy Variations

- **Seafood Chow Fun:** Substitute beef with shrimp or scallops (ensure they are patted dry).
- **Vegetarian:** Use sliced king oyster mushrooms or extra-firm tofu and substitute oyster sauce with vegetarian mushroom sauce.
- **Spicy:** Add a teaspoon of XO sauce or chili crisp during the final toss.

## Storage and Reheating

- **Storage:** Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
- **Reheating:** Avoid the microwave if possible, as it can make noodles rubbery. Re-fry in a hot pan with a teaspoon of water to loosen the noodles.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the difference between Chow Fun and Chow Mein?** Chow Fun uses wide, flat rice noodles (Ho Fun), whereas Chow Mein uses thin, wheat-based egg noodles.

**Can I use dried rice noodles?** While fresh is preferred for texture, you can use dried wide rice noodles. Soak them in warm water until pliable, but be aware they lack the silkiness of fresh Ho Fun.

**How do I get *Wok Hei* at home?** Use a carbon steel wok, get it screaming hot before adding oil, and don't be afraid of a little char on the noodles.
