Moo Goo Gai Pan is a classic Cantonese stir-fry featuring tender chicken breast and sliced button mushrooms in a light, savory white sauce. This 30-minute recipe utilizes the "velveting" technique to guarantee juicy chicken, replicating the delicate texture of restaurant-quality takeout without deep frying. By using a wok or large skillet over high heat, home cooks can achieve an authentic, healthy dinner that serves four, emphasizing fresh vegetables like snow peas and bamboo shoots.

## **What Is** Moo Goo Gai Pan?

Moo Goo Gai Pan (蘑菇鸡片) translates literally to "mushroom chicken slices." Originating from Cantonese cuisine, it is a staple in Chinese-American restaurants. Unlike deep-fried dishes drenched in heavy sweet sauces, this dish is defined by its healthy profile and clean flavors. It features thinly sliced chicken breast, button mushrooms, and crisp vegetables enrobed in a translucent, savory white sauce typically made from chicken stock, ginger, and oyster sauce.

![Moo Goo Gai Pan in white bowl with glossy white sauce, tender velveted chicken, mushrooms, snow peas, bamboo shoots, water chestnuts, and green onion garnish](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Moo-Goo-Gai-Pan-3a-1536.webp)

## **Why This Recipe Works**

Developing a 30-minute Moo Goo Gai Pan that rivals takeout required specific testing to solve the common problem of dry, rubbery chicken breast.

**Recipe Testing Notes:**

- **Batch 1 (Direct Fry):** We sliced chicken and added it directly to the wok. The exterior seared well, but the breast meat dried out before the vegetables were cooked. *Result: Failure.*
- **Batch 2 (Baking Soda Tenderizing):** We used a baking soda bath for the meat. While tender, the chicken developed a slightly mushy texture and soapy aftertaste, requiring extensive rinsing. *Result: Subpar.*
- **Batch 3 (Velveting with Egg White):** We employed the classic restaurant technique of marinating meat in egg white, cornstarch, and rice wine. The chicken was silky and moist, but the prep time exceeded 30 minutes due to marinating wait times. *Result: Good, but too slow.*
- **Batch 4 (Quick Velveting - Optimal Result):** We shortened the marinating time to just 10 minutes while prepping vegetables and used a "pass through" hot oil method (or sear method). The cornstarch in the marinade protected the meat fibers, locking in moisture. *Result: Perfect texture in under 30 minutes.*

## **Ingredients You'll Need For** Moo Goo Gai Pan

![Moo Goo Gai Pan sauce ingredients in small bowls: chicken broth, oyster sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and cornstarch arranged on bamboo surface](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Moo-Goo-Gai-Pan-b-1536.webp)

- **1 lb Chicken Breast:** Sliced thinly against the grain.
- **8 oz Button Mushrooms:** Sliced; the star vegetable.
- **1 cup Snow Peas:** Trimmed; adds crunch.
- **1 can (8 oz) Bamboo Shoots:** Drained and sliced; traditional texture.
- **1 can (8 oz) Water Chestnuts:** Drained; adds crispness.
- **Marinade:** 1 tbsp Rice Wine (Shaoxing), 1 tsp Cornstarch, 1 Egg White (optional, or 1 tbsp water), ½ tsp Salt.
- **Sauce:** ½ cup Chicken Broth (low sodium), 1 tbsp Oyster Sauce, 1 tsp Soy Sauce (light), ½ tsp Sugar, 1 tsp Cornstarch (for slurry).
- **Aromatics:** 2 cloves Garlic (minced), 1 tsp Fresh Ginger (minced), 2 Green Onions (chopped).

## **Equipment**

- **Wok or Large Heavy Skillet:** We tested with both a traditional carbon steel wok and a stainless steel skillet. The wok provided the best "wok hei" (breath of the wok) and smoky flavor. A heavy skillet retains heat well if you do not own a wok.
- **Sharp Chef’s Knife:** Essential for slicing chicken breast thinly and safely.
- **Mixing Bowls:** Two medium bowls for the marinade and sauce mixture.

## **How to Make** Moo Goo Gai Pan **(Step-by-Step)**

1. **Prepare the Chicken:** Slice the chicken breast against the grain into thin, bite-sized strips. In a bowl, combine the chicken with the marinade ingredients (rice wine, cornstarch, egg white/water, salt). Mix well and set aside for 10 minutes while you chop the vegetables.
  ![Slicing chicken breast against the grain for Moo Goo Gai Pan, with velveted chicken strips in egg white cornstarch marinade](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Moo-Goo-Gai-Pan-c-1536.webp)
2. **Make the Sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together the chicken broth, oyster sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and 1 teaspoon of cornstarch until smooth. Ensure no lumps remain.
  ![Whisked Moo Goo Gai Pan sauce in white bowl with metal whisk, smooth glossy light brown mixture with no lumps](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Moo-Goo-Gai-Pan-d-1536.webp)
3. **Sear the Chicken:** Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat with 1 tablespoon of oil. Add the marinated chicken in a single layer. Sear for 2–3 minutes until browned and mostly cooked through. Remove the chicken from the wok and set aside on a plate.
  ![Velveted chicken strips searing in carbon steel wok for Moo Goo Gai Pan, golden brown edges with slightly pink centers Why it works:](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Moo-Goo-Gai-Pan-3-1536.webp)
4. **Stir-Fry Vegetables:** Add another tablespoon of oil to the hot wok. Add the mushrooms first and stir-fry for 1 minute until they begin to soften. Add the snow peas, bamboo shoots, water chestnuts, garlic, and ginger. Stir-fry for another 2 minutes.
  ![Stir-frying mushrooms, snow peas, bamboo shoots, and water chestnuts with garlic and ginger in carbon steel wok for Moo Goo Gai Pan](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Moo-Goo-Gai-Pan-2-1536.webp)
5. **Combine and Sauce:** Return the cooked chicken to the wok. Give the sauce mixture a quick whisk (cornstarch settles) and pour it over the chicken and vegetables.
  ![Combining seared chicken and vegetables with glossy white sauce in carbon steel wok for Moo Goo Gai Pan](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Moo-Goo-Gai-Pan-1-1536.webp)
6. **Thicken and Serve:** Toss everything continuously for 1–2 minutes. The sauce will bubble and thicken rapidly, coating all ingredients. Stir in the green onions. Remove from heat immediately.
  ![Finished Moo Goo Gai Pan in white bowl with glossy white sauce, tender velveted chicken, mushrooms, snow peas, bamboo shoots, water chestnuts, and green onion garnish](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Moo-Goo-Gai-Pan-3a-1536.webp)

## **Pro Tips For  Success**

- **Slice Against the Grain:** Cutting the chicken breast perpendicular to the muscle fibers shortens the fibers, making the meat significantly more tender.
- **Mise en Place:** Stir-frying happens fast. Have all ingredients chopped and the sauce mixed before you turn on the stove.
- **High Heat is Key:** Do not crowd the pan, and keep the heat high. This ensures the food sears rather than steaming in its own juices.

## **Common Mistakes to Avoid**

- **Overcooking the Chicken:** Since the meat is sliced thin, it cooks in minutes. Overcooking results in a rubbery texture. Remove it from the wok while slightly pink if unsure; it will finish cooking in the residual heat and sauce.
- **Soggy Vegetables:** Add hard vegetables like carrots or broccoli earlier, but add snow peas and water chestnuts near the end to maintain their snap.
- **Lumpy Sauce:** Always whisk the cornstarch into the cold liquid ingredients *before* adding them to the heat. Adding dry cornstarch directly to a hot wok creates unpalatable lumps.

## **Easy Variations**

- **Spicy Moo Goo Gai Pan:** Add 1 teaspoon of chili garlic sauce (Sambal Oelek) or dried red chili peppers to the wok along with the ginger and garlic.
- **Vegetarian Version:** Substitute the chicken with extra-firm tofu cubes (pressed and patted dry) and use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.
- **Low Carb/Keto:** Replace the cornstarch in the sauce with xanthan gum and serve over cauliflower rice instead of white rice.

## **Serving Suggestions**

Serve this dish immediately over steamed white jasmine rice or brown rice to soak up the savory sauce. For a low-carb option, serve over cauliflower rice or shirataki noodles. A side of hot and sour soup completes the meal.

## **Storage and Reheating**

- **Storage:** Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. The vegetables will soften slightly but retain flavor.
- **Reheating:** Reheat in a skillet over medium heat for 2–3 minutes. Avoid the microwave if possible, as it can make the chicken rubbery; however, if used, cover with a damp paper towel to retain moisture.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

**1. Can I use chicken thighs instead of chicken breast?**  
Yes, boneless skinless chicken thighs work well and offer more fat, which some prefer for flavor. However, reduce the marinating time slightly, as thighs are naturally more tender than breasts.

**2. What is "velveting" chicken?**  
Velveting is a Chinese cooking technique where meat is marinated in a mixture of cornstarch, egg white, and rice wine. It creates a protective barrier that seals in moisture, ensuring the meat remains silky and tender even under high heat. 

**3. Is Moo Goo Gai Pan gluten-free?**  
Traditionally, it is not, as soy sauce and oyster sauce contain wheat. However, you can easily make it gluten-free by substituting regular soy sauce with tamari or gluten-free soy sauce and ensuring your oyster sauce is labeled GF.

**4. Can I use other vegetables?**  
Absolutely. While mushrooms are standard, you can add broccoli florets, sliced bell peppers, baby corn, or bok choy. Just adjust cooking times accordingly—harder vegetables need more time in the wok.

**5. Why is my sauce runny?**  
If your sauce is runny, it likely did not reach a high enough boil to activate the cornstarch. Ensure the wok is very hot when you pour the sauce in, and stir constantly until it thickens to a glossy consistency. If it remains thin, mix a little more cornstarch with water and drizzle it in while stirring.
