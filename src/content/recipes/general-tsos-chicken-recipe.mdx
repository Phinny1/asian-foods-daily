## **What Is General Tso’s Chicken?**

Before we get into the recipe, let’s talk a little about the dish itself. General Tso’s Chicken is a staple in Chinese-American cuisine, known for its deep-fried chicken pieces tossed in a glossy, sweet, and slightly spicy sauce. Despite its popularity in the U.S., you might be surprised to learn that it’s not a traditional dish in China. In fact, it was created in the 1950s by a Taiwanese chef who named it after a famous Chinese military leader, General Tso. Over the years, it has become a symbol of the fusion between Chinese flavors and American tastes.

The magic of General Tso’s [Chicken lies in its sauce—a perfect](https://www.asianfoodsdaily.com/recipes/how-to-make-perfect-chinese-chicken-salad/) harmony of soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, garlic, ginger, and a kick of red pepper flakes. Paired with crispy chicken and garnished with green onions, it’s a dish that’s as visually appealing as it is delicious.

## **Ingredients You’ll Need**



To make General Tso’s Chicken, you’ll need a mix of pantry staples and a few special ingredients. Don’t worry—most of these are easy to find at your local grocery store. Here’s what you’ll need:

- **Chicken**: 1 lb of boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces. (Thighs are juicier, but breasts work too!)
- **Cornstarch**: ½ cup, for coating the chicken and achieving that crispy texture.
- **Soy Sauce**: ¼ cup, for both the marinade and the sauce. (Use low-sodium if you prefer.)
- **Rice Vinegar**: 2 tablespoons, for a tangy kick.
- **Brown Sugar**: 3 tablespoons, to balance the savory and spicy flavors.
- **Garlic**: 3 cloves, minced.
- **Ginger**: 1 tablespoon, freshly grated.
- **Red Pepper Flakes**: 1 teaspoon (adjust to your spice preference).
- **Green Onions**: 2-3 stalks, chopped, for garnish.
- **Vegetable Oil**: For frying the chicken.
- **Sesame Seeds** (optional): For an extra touch of flavor and presentation.

### **For the Marinade:**

- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch

### **For the Sauce:**

- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 3 tablespoons brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (for thickening)

## **Step-by-Step Instructions**

Now, let’s get to the fun part—cooking! Follow these simple steps to create your own General Tso’s Chicken:

### **1. Marinate the Chicken**

- In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces with 2 tablespoons of soy sauce, 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar, and 1 tablespoon of cornstarch. Mix well and let it sit for 15-20 minutes. This step helps tenderize the chicken and prepares it for that crispy coating.

### **2. Coat and Fry the Chicken**

- After marinating, coat the chicken pieces in the remaining cornstarch, making sure each piece is evenly covered.
- Heat about 1 inch of vegetable oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Once hot, fry the chicken in batches until golden brown and crispy (about 3-4 minutes per side). Be careful not to overcrowd the pan!
- Transfer the fried chicken to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

### **3. Make the Sauce**

- In a separate pan or wok, heat a tablespoon of oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, sautéing for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
- Stir in the soy sauce, rice vinegar, brown sugar, and red pepper flakes. Let the mixture simmer for 2-3 minutes.
- To thicken the sauce, add the cornstarch-water mixture and stir until the sauce becomes glossy and slightly thick.

### **4. Toss and Serve**

- Add the fried chicken to the sauce and toss until each piece is evenly coated.
- Garnish with chopped green onions and sesame seeds (if using).
- Serve hot with steamed rice or your favorite stir-fried vegetables.



## **Tips and Variations**

- **Adjust the Spice**: If you love heat, add more red pepper flakes or even a dash of hot sauce. For a milder version, reduce the pepper flakes or omit them altogether.
- **Make It Vegetarian**: Swap the chicken for tofu or cauliflower for a delicious vegetarian twist.
- **Healthier Option**: Instead of frying, you can bake the chicken at 400°F for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway through. It won’t be as crispy, but it’s a lighter alternative.
- **Double the Sauce**: If you’re a sauce lover (like me!), feel free to double the sauce ingredients for extra flavor.
