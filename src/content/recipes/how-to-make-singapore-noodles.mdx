Singapore Noodles, or “Sing Chow Mai Fun,” are a delicious stir-fried noodle dish that has captured the hearts (and stomachs) of food lovers around the world. ## **What Are Singapore Noodles?**

Singapore Noodles are a vibrant and flavorful dish made with rice vermicelli noodles, curry powder, shrimp, char siu (barbecued pork), and a medley of vegetables. The [noodles are stir-fried to perfection](https://asianfoodsdaily.com/how-to-make-perfect-garlic-noodles/), creating a harmonious blend of textures and tastes. The curry powder gives the dish its signature yellow hue and a subtle spicy kick, while the shrimp and char siu add protein and depth of flavor. It’s a dish that’s as visually appealing as it is delicious, making it a favorite in many households and restaurants alike.

## **The History of Singapore Noodles**

Despite the name, Singapore Noodles are not a traditional dish from Singapore. In fact, if you ask for Singapore Noodles in Singapore, you might get some confused looks. The dish is believed to have originated in Hong Kong, where it was created as a way to cater to Western tastes. The name “Singapore” was likely used to evoke a sense of exoticism and to differentiate it from other noodle dishes. Over time, Singapore Noodles have become a staple in Chinese restaurants worldwide, loved for their unique flavor profile and versatility.

## **How to Make Singapore Noodles**

Now, I know what you’re thinking: “This sounds amazing, but can I really make it at home?” Absolutely! And trust me, it’s easier than you might think. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

![Singapore Noodles Ingredients](https://spcdn.shortpixel.ai/spio/ret_img,q_cdnize,to_auto,s_webp:avif/asianfoodsdaily.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/Singapore-Rice-Noodles-I-300x300.png)

### **Ingredients:**

- 200g rice vermicelli
- 150g shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 100g char siu pork, sliced thinly (substitute with ham or chicken if needed)
- 1 small onion, sliced
- 1 bell pepper, sliced
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 2 tablespoons curry powder
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: chopped green onions or cilantro for garnish

### **Instructions:**

1. **Prep the Noodles:** Soak the rice vermicelli in hot water for about 5 minutes until softened. Drain and set aside.
2. **Cook the Eggs:** Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Pour in the beaten eggs and scramble them lightly. Remove and set aside.
3. **Stir-Fry the Protein and Veggies:** In the same pan, add another tablespoon of oil. Toss in the shrimp and cook until pink. Add the char siu pork, onion, and bell pepper. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until the veggies are slightly tender.
4. **Season and Combine:** Sprinkle the curry powder over the mixture and stir well. Add the noodles, scrambled eggs, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Toss everything together until evenly coated and heated through.
5. **Serve:** Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with green onions or cilantro if you’re feeling fancy.

And there you have it—homemade Singapore Noodles that’ll make you feel like a culinary rockstar. Bonus points if you serve them in those cute takeout boxes for the full experience! Pro tip: the first time I made this, I accidentally used too much curry powder and turned my kitchen into a spice bomb. Lesson learned—measure twice, season once!

## **Common Mistakes to Avoid**

Even the most experienced cooks can make mistakes when trying a new dish. Here are some common pitfalls to watch out for:

- **Overcooking the noodles**: This can make them mushy and unappetizing.
- **Using too much curry powder**: A little goes a long way; too much can overpower the other flavors.
- **Not prepping ingredients ahead of time**: Stir-frying is a fast process, so have everything ready to go.
- **Forgetting to season**: Taste as you go and adjust the seasoning as needed.

By avoiding these mistakes, you’ll be well on your way to creating a delicious plate of Singapore Noodles.

## **Variations of Singapore Noodles**

While the classic Singapore Noodles recipe is beloved, there are many variations you can try. Some people like to add chicken or beef instead of shrimp and char siu. Vegetarians can omit the meat altogether and add more vegetables or tofu. You can also experiment with different spices or sauces to create your own unique version. The possibilities are endless! Why stick to the script when you can write your own culinary story?

## **Nutritional Benefits**

While Singapore Noodles are undoubtedly delicious, they also offer some nutritional benefits. The dish is typically high in protein, thanks to the shrimp and char siu, and the vegetables provide essential vitamins and minerals. The rice vermicelli noodles are also a good source of carbohydrates, providing energy for your day. However, like any dish, moderation is key. The curry powder and soy sauce can add sodium, so it’s best to enjoy Singapore Noodles as part of a balanced diet.
