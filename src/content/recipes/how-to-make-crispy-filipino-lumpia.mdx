**Filipino lumpia** is a crispy, deep-fried spring roll filled with seasoned ground pork, vegetables, and aromatics, wrapped in a thin lumpia wrapper and fried until golden brown. To make crispy lumpia: mix ground pork with garlic, onion, carrots, and soy sauce → fill and roll wrappers tightly → seal with egg wash → fry at 350°F (175°C) for 3–4 minutes per side until deeply golden. Serve with sweet chili dipping sauce.

## What Is **Filipino lumpia**?

Lumpia Shanghai is the Filipino version of the spring roll — thinner, crispier, and more savory than its Chinese or Vietnamese cousins. It's a staple at birthday parties, fiestas, and family gatherings across the Philippines and Filipino diaspora communities worldwide, right alongside beloved comfort dishes like [Filipino pork sour soup (Sinigang na Baboy)](https://www.asianfoodsdaily.com/recipes/sinigang-na-baboy-recipe/) and chicken adobo. As [Smithsonian Magazine documents in its exploration of Filipino cuisine](https://www.smithsonianmag.com/arts-culture/filipino-cuisine-asian-fusion-180954947/), lumpia arrived with Chinese traders in the pre-colonial era, evolving from Fujianese popiah into something distinctly Filipino — smaller, meatier, and always served at celebrations. The filling is meat-forward (typically pork), the wrapper is paper-thin, and the fry is always deep — never pan-fried if you want authentic crunch.

## Why This Recipe Works

**Recipe Testing Notes:**

- **Batch 1 (Failure):** Used egg roll wrappers instead of lumpia wrappers. Result: too thick, chewy texture, wrapper separated during frying.
- **Batch 2 (Failure):** Added too much water to the filling. Result: steam burst wrappers open mid-fry.
- **Batch 3 (Alternative tried):** Substituted ground chicken. Result: acceptable but drier — required an extra teaspoon of sesame oil.
- **Batch 4 (Optimal):** Ground pork (80/20 fat ratio) + fine-diced carrots and water chestnuts + cold filling before rolling + frying at exactly 350°F. Result: maximum crunch, juicy interior, no blowouts.

**Why it works:** Cold filling reduces steam pressure. Tight rolling prevents oil seepage. A 350°F oil temperature ensures the wrapper crisps before the filling overcooks.

## **Filipino lumpia** Ingredients

*(Makes approximately 30 pieces)*

- 1 lb ground pork (80/20)
- 1 cup carrots, finely shredded
- ½ cup water chestnuts, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 small onion, finely diced
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp oyster sauce
- ½ tsp black pepper
- 1 egg (for binding the filling)
- 1 egg (beaten, for sealing wrappers)
- 30 lumpia wrappers (thawed if frozen)
- Neutral oil for deep frying (vegetable or canola)

## Common Substitutions

- **Ground pork → Ground chicken or turkey** (add 1 tsp sesame oil for moisture)
- **Water chestnuts → Jicama or cabbage** (drain well to avoid wet filling)
- **Lumpia wrappers → Popiah wrappers** (closest alternative; avoid egg roll wrappers)
- **Oyster sauce → Hoisin sauce** (slightly sweeter but works well)

## Equipment *(Annotated Tool Recommendations Based on Testing)*

- **Deep, heavy-bottomed pot or wok** — Retains heat better than a thin skillet; prevents oil temperature from dropping when you add lumpia
- **Instant-read thermometer** — Non-negotiable; batch failures occurred every time oil temperature was estimated rather than measured
- **Slotted spoon or spider strainer** — Drains oil efficiently without crushing the crispy wrapper
- **Wire cooling rack over a sheet pan** — Keeps lumpia from steaming on paper towels and going soggy
- **Plastic cutting board or parchment** — Ideal surface for rolling; prevents wrappers from sticking

## **How to Make Filipino lumpia(Step-by-Step)**

1. **Make the filling.** Combine ground pork, carrots, water chestnuts, garlic, onion, soy sauce, oyster sauce, pepper, and one egg. Mix until just combined. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
2. **Prep your station.** Lay out wrappers, beaten egg, a small brush, and your wire rack.
3. **Fill the wrapper.** Place one wrapper in a diamond shape. Add 1½ tbsp of filling in a thin log shape near the bottom edge — about the thickness of your thumb.
4. **Roll tightly.** Fold the bottom edge over the filling, tuck in the sides, and roll upward. Seal the top corner with beaten egg. Press gently to seal.
5. **Heat the oil.** Pour 2–3 inches of oil into your pot. Heat to 350°F. No thermometer? Dip the tip of a wooden chopstick into the oil — it's ready when small bubbles form around it immediately.
6. **Fry in batches.** Add 6–8 lumpia at a time. Fry 3–4 minutes per side until golden brown and crispy.
7. **Drain and rest.** Transfer to a wire rack. Wait 2 minutes before serving.

> ⚠️ **Safety Note:** Never add water to hot oil — it causes violent splattering. If a wrapper bursts, reduce heat immediately and step back. When done, allow the oil to cool completely before disposing of it; never pour hot oil down the drain.

## Pro Tips

- **Always refrigerate your filling** before rolling — warm filling creates steam that bursts wrappers
- **Roll tight, roll thin** — Loose rolls absorb more oil and fry unevenly
- **Freeze uncooked lumpia** in a single layer first, then bag them — fry directly from frozen (add 1–2 extra minutes)
- **Double-wrap** for extra crunch on special occasions

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Using egg roll wrappers (too thick)
- Overfilling the wrapper (causes blowouts)
- Frying at too low a temperature (results in greasy lumpia)
- Draining on paper towels (steam = soggy bottoms)

## Storage and Reheating

- **Uncooked lumpia:** Freeze on a baking sheet in a single layer, then store in zip bags up to 3 months
- **Cooked leftovers:** Refrigerate up to 3 days in an airtight container
- **Reheating:** Air fryer at 375°F for 4–5 minutes restores crispiness; avoid microwaving

## Frequently Asked Questions

**1. What is the difference between lumpia and egg rolls?** Lumpia uses a much thinner wrapper than Chinese-American egg rolls, resulting in a crispier, more delicate exterior. The filling is also typically simpler and more meat-forward.

**2. Can I bake lumpia instead of frying?** Yes, but the result is noticeably less crispy. Bake at 400°F for 20–25 minutes, flipping halfway, and brush with oil for best results.

**3. Why does my lumpia keep bursting open during frying?** The most common cause is overfilling, a loose seal, or wet filling. Refrigerate the filling before rolling and apply egg wash generously to the seam.

**4. What wrappers are best for lumpia?** Look for **Spring Home TYJ Spring Roll Pastry** or any brand labeled "lumpia wrapper" or "spring roll wrapper" — thin, starchy, and semi-translucent. Found in Asian grocery stores.

**5. Can lumpia be made ahead of time?** Yes. Roll and freeze uncooked lumpia up to 3 months. Fry directly from frozen — no thawing needed.
