Kung Pao Chicken (*Gong Bao Ji Ding*) is a world-renowned Szechuan stir-fry featuring tender diced chicken, crunchy roasted peanuts, and dried chilies. This recipe prioritizes the "Ma-La" (numbing and spicy) profile, balanced by a sophisticated sweet-and-sour glaze.

![Authentic Szechuan Kung Pao Chicken (Gong Bao Ji Ding) with glossy chicken cubes, roasted peanuts, dried red chilies, and scallions in white bowl](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Kung-Pao-Chicken-1536.webp)

## What Is Kung Pao Chicken?

Kung Pao Chicken is a cornerstone of Szechuan cuisine, named after Ding Baozhen, a Qing Dynasty official. Authentically known as *Gong Bao Ji Ding*, the dish is defined by its complex layers of flavor: the numbing sensation of Szechuan peppercorns, the smoky heat of charred chilies, and the "Lychee-flavored" sauce—a traditional Szechuan culinary term for a specific balance of sweet, sour, and salty.

## Why This *Gong Bao Ji Ding* Recipe Works

- **Velveting Technique:** Using a cornstarch and egg white marinade ensures the chicken remains silky and tender even under high heat.
- **Layered Aromatics:** By frying the peppercorns and chilies first, we infuse the oil with the dish's signature "Ma-La" essence.
- **The Golden Ratio:** This recipe uses a precise 1:1:1 ratio of soy sauce, sugar, and vinegar to mimic the authentic restaurant profile.

## Ingredients You’ll Need For Kung Pao Chicken

![Mise en place ingredients for authentic Kung Pao Chicken on bamboo cutting board: diced chicken, dried red chilies, roasted peanuts, green onions, garlic, ginger, and Kung Pao sauce](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Kung-Pao-Chicken-1-1536.webp)

**The Chicken & Marinade**

- 1 lb (450g) Chicken thigh, boneless/skinless, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- 1 tbsp Light soy sauce
- 1 tsp Cornstarch
- 1 tsp Toasted sesame oil

**The Kung Pao Sauce**

- 2 tbsp Chinkiang black vinegar (essential for authentic flavor)
- 1 tbsp Light soy sauce
- 1 tsp Dark soy sauce (for color)
- 1 tbsp Granulated sugar
- 1 tsp Cornstarch
- 2 tbsp Water or chicken stock

**Aromatics & Crunch**

- 10–15 Dried red chilies (snip in half for more heat)
- 1 tsp Whole Szechuan peppercorns
- 3 cloves Garlic, thinly sliced
- 1-inch Ginger, minced
- 2 Scallions, whites and greens separated, cut into 1-inch rounds
- 1/2 cup Roasted unsalted peanuts

## Equipment

- **Carbon Steel Wok:** Preferred for achieving "Wok Hei" (breath of the wok).
- **High-Heat Spatula:** For rapid tossing.
- **Small Glass Bowls:** For "Mise en place" (essential for fast stir-frying).

## How to Make Kung Pao Chicken (Step-by-Step)

![Chicken cubes marinating in soy sauce and Shaoxing wine for Kung Pao Chicken, showing velveting technique with glossy coating](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Kung-Pao-Chicken-2-1536.webp)

1. **Marinate the Chicken:** Combine chicken cubes with the marinade ingredients. Let sit for at least 20 minutes.
2. **Prepare the Sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together the vinegar, soy sauces, sugar, cornstarch, and water. Set aside.
3. **Infuse the Oil:** Heat 2 tbsp of neutral oil in the wok over medium heat. Add Szechuan peppercorns and dried chilies. Fry until the chilies turn dark red (do not burn).
4. **Sear the Chicken:** Increase heat to high. Add the chicken in a single layer. Let it sear for 1 minute, then stir-fry until 80% cooked through.
5. **Add Aromatics:** Toss in the garlic, ginger, and the white parts of the scallions. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
6. **Glaze:** Give the sauce mixture a quick stir and pour it into the center of the wok. Stir rapidly as the sauce thickens and coats the chicken.
7. **The Finish:** Fold in the roasted peanuts and the green parts of the scallions. Toss for another 30 seconds.
8. **Serve:** Transfer immediately to a plate to prevent the chicken from overcooking.

## Pro Tips For Perfect Kung Pao Chicken

- **Toast the Peppercorns:** For maximum aroma, lightly toast peppercorns in a dry pan and crush them slightly before adding to the oil.
- **Control the Heat:** If you prefer a milder dish, leave the dried chilies whole. If you want maximum spice, snip them to release the seeds.
- **Room Temperature Meat:** Never stir-fry ice-cold chicken; let it sit at room temperature for 10 minutes to ensure even cooking.

![Pouring Kung Pao sauce over seared chicken and dried chilies in wok to create glossy lychee-flavored glaze](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Kung-Pao-Chicken-4-1536.webp)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Crowding the Wok:** If cooking for a large group, cook the chicken in batches. Crowding causes the meat to steam rather than sear.
- **Burning the Chilies:** Dried chilies turn bitter if blackened. Keep them moving and adjust heat as necessary.
- **Skipping the Black Vinegar:** Regular balsamic or white vinegar lacks the smoky depth of Chinkiang vinegar.

## Easy Variations

- **Kung Pao Shrimp:** Replace chicken with jumbo shrimp; reduce cooking time to 2-3 minutes total.
- **Vegetarian/Vegan:** Use extra-firm pressed tofu or cauliflower florets.
- **Extra Veggies:** Add diced bell peppers or celery for additional crunch and volume.

## Serving Suggestions

Serve Kung Pao Chicken alongside:

- **Steamed Jasmine Rice:** To soak up the savory sauce.
- **Smashed Cucumber Salad:** A cold, acidic contrast to the heat.
- **Blanched Bok Choy:** To add a fresh, bitter element to the meal.

## Storage and Reheating

- **Storage:** Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
- **Reheating:** Reheat in a skillet over medium heat with a splash of water to loosen the sauce. Avoid the microwave if possible, as it can make the peanuts soggy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What can I use instead of Shaoxing wine?** Dry sherry is the best substitute. If avoiding alcohol, use chicken stock with a drop of lime juice.

**Why is my sauce too thick?** The cornstarch may have over-activated. Thin it out with a tablespoon of water or chicken stock while the wok is still over heat.

**Is Kung Pao Chicken gluten-free?** Standard soy sauce contains wheat. To make this gluten-free, substitute with Tamari or Coconut Aminos.
