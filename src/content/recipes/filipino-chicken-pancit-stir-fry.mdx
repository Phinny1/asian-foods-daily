**Filipino Chicken Pancit Stir-Fry** is a traditional noodle dish consisting of rice stick noodles (bihon) sautéed with sliced chicken, vegetables (cabbage, carrots, and celery), and a savory broth infused with soy sauce and aromatics. This recipe simplifies the classic technique by using boneless chicken thighs for tenderness and a specific soaking method to guarantee perfectly chewy, non-sticky noodles every time.

![Filipino Chicken Pancit Canton stir-fry with glossy noodles, tender chicken thighs, julienned carrots, cabbage, and calamansi wedges on white plate, garnished with black pepper](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Chicken-Pancit-1-1536.webp)

## **What Is Chicken Pancit ?**

Pancit is a staple in Filipino cuisine, brought to the Philippines by Chinese traders and adapted into a national favorite. While there are many variations like Pancit Canton (egg noodles) and Pancit Palabok (thin rice noodles with shrimp sauce), **Pancit Bihon** is the most common household version. It is often served at birthdays and celebrations to symbolize long life. This stir-fry version features a dry, savory finish rather than a soupy consistency, allowing the flavor of the broth to absorb fully into the noodles.

## **Why This Recipe Works**

Through extensive kitchen testing, we determined the precise hydration and heat balance required for authentic texture.

**Recipe Testing Notes:**

- **Batch 1 (Boiled Noodles):** We attempted to boil the noodles like pasta before stir-frying. The result was a gummy, broken texture that clumped together immediately. **Verdict:** Failure.
- **Batch 2 (Chicken Breast):** Using breast meat resulted in dry, stringy pieces after the high-heat stir-fry. **Verdict:** Subpar.
- **Batch 3 (Broth Hydration):** We soaked the noodles in cold water for 30 minutes before adding them to the simmering broth. The noodles absorbed the chicken stock and soy sauce evenly, achieving a distinct "al dente" bite without clumping. Using chicken thighs ensured juicy meat. **Verdict:** Optimal Result.

## **Ingredients You'll Need For Chicken Pancit**

- **8 oz Pancit Bihon (Rice Sticks):** Do not substitute with egg noodles for this specific recipe.
- **1 lb Chicken Thighs:** Boneless, skinless, sliced into thin strips.
- **4 cups Chicken Broth:** Low sodium preferred to control saltiness.
- **2 cups Cabbage:** Shredded or chopped.
- **1 cup Carrots:** Julienned.
- **1 medium Onion:** Sliced.
- **4 cloves Garlic:** Minced.
- **1/4 cup Soy Sauce:** For color and base flavor.
- **1 tbsp Oyster Sauce:** For depth and umami.
- **1 tbsp Cooking Oil:** High smoke point oil (canola or vegetable).

## **Equipment**

- **Large Wok or Heavy Skillet:** Essential for high-heat stir-frying; a standard 12-inch skillet works if a wok is unavailable.
- **Sharp Chef’s Knife:** Required for uniformly slicing chicken and vegetables to ensure even cooking.
- **Tongs:** The best tool for tossing the delicate rice noodles without breaking them.
- **Measuring Cups/Liquid Measure:** Critical for achieving the correct noodle-to-broth ratio.

## **How to Make Chicken Pancit  (Step-by-Step)**

1. **Prep the Noodles:** Place the dry rice noodles in a large bowl and cover with cold water. Soak for 15 to 20 minutes until pliable but not soft. Drain well.
  ![Dry rice noodles soaking in clear water in white ceramic bowl, translucent pancit bihon noodles softening for Filipino stir-fry recipe, bright natural window light](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Chicken-Pancit-7-1536.webp)
2. **Sauté Aromatics and Chicken:** Heat oil in a large wok over medium-high heat. Sauté onions and garlic until fragrant. Add the sliced chicken thighs and cook until browned on the outside.
3. **Build the Broth:** Pour in the chicken broth, soy sauce, and oyster sauce. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil.
  ![Dark soy sauce and chicken broth pouring into carbon steel wok with golden seared chicken thighs and sautéed onions, building savory sauce for Filipino panci](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Chicken-Pancit-4-1536.webp)
4. **Cook the Noodles:** Add the drained noodles to the boiling broth. Using tongs, toss constantly for 2–3 minutes. The noodles should absorb most of the liquid.
  ![Metal tongs tossing white rice noodles with chicken and savory sauce in carbon steel wok, cooking Filipino Chicken Pancit Canton stir-fry, glossy noodle texture](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Chicken-Pancit-3-1536.webp)
5. **Add Vegetables:** Once the noodles are tender but still chewy, add the carrots and cabbage. Continue tossing for another 2 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp.
6. **Season and Serve:** Season with ground black pepper and fish sauce to taste. Serve immediately with calamansi or lemon wedges.
  ![Authentic Filipino Chicken Pancit Canton recipe finished dish with savory stir-fried noodles, juicy chicken pieces, fresh vegetables, and calamansi lime garnish on white serving plate](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Chicken-Pancit-1-1536.webp)

## **Pro Tips For  Perfect Chicken Pancit**

- **Don't Over-Soak:** Soaking noodles for longer than 30 minutes makes them too fragile for stir-frying.
- **High Heat:** Keep the heat high to ensure the broth evaporates quickly, leaving the noodles dry and savory rather than soggy.
- **Toss, Don't Stir:** Use a lifting motion with tongs to coat the noodles; stirring mashes the starch and creates clumps.

## **Common Mistakes to Avoid**

- **Soggy Noodles:** This usually happens if too much broth is used or the heat is too low. Stick to the 4-cup broth ratio for 8 oz of noodles.
- **Mushy Vegetables:** Add the cabbage last. Overcooked cabbage releases water and ruins the texture.
- **Bland Flavor:** Rice noodles are naturally tasteless. Ensure the broth is well-seasoned before adding the noodles, or the final dish will be flat.

## **Easy Variations**

- **Pancit Canton:** Swap rice noodles for wheat flour noodles (canton) and reduce broth slightly, as wheat noodles absorb less liquid.
- **Seafood Pancit:** Replace chicken with shrimp. Add the shrimp in the last 3 minutes of cooking to prevent rubberiness.
- **Vegetarian:** Use vegetable broth and replace chicken with firm tofu cubes or extra shiitake mushrooms.

## **Serving Suggestions**

Pancit is often eaten as a standalone meal but pairs excellently with other Filipino favorites. Serve alongside **Lumpiang Shanghai** (Filipino spring rolls) or with a side of white rice for a carb-heavy feast. A squeeze of **calamansi** (Filipino lime) over the noodles right before eating is non-negotiable for authentic flavor.

## **Storage and Reheating**

Store leftover Pancit in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Rice noodles harden when cold. To reheat, sprinkle a tablespoon of water over the noodles and microwave for 1 minute, or sauté in a pan with a splash of broth to rehydrate them. Freezing is not recommended as it alters the noodle texture significantly.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**

**1. What is the difference between Pancit Bihon and Pancit Canton?**  
Pancit Bihon uses thin rice noodles, resulting in a lighter, more delicate texture, while Pancit Canton uses wheat egg noodles, which are thicker, chewier, and have a distinct yellow color.

**2. Can I make Chicken Pancit ahead of time?**  
Yes, but it is best served fresh. If making ahead, undercook the vegetables slightly so they retain texture when reheated. Store the dish without the calamansi garnish.

**3. Why do my rice noodles stick together?**  
Noodles usually stick because they were over-soaked, the heat was too low during stir-frying, or they were stirred too aggressively. Use tongs to toss and keep the heat high to separate the strands.

**4. Is Pancit gluten-free?**  
Traditional Pancit Bihon rice noodles are gluten-free, but the soy sauce and broth often contain wheat. To make it gluten-free, substitute soy sauce with tamari or coconut aminos and use certified gluten-free broth.

**5. What can I substitute for calamansi?**  
If calamansi is unavailable, a mix of lemon and lime juice provides the closest acidity and aromatic profile to mimic the unique citrus flavor of the Philippines.

## **Final Note**

Mastering Filipino Chicken Pancit Stir-Fry is about balancing hydration and heat. By pre-soaking the noodles and using chicken thighs for richness, you elevate a humble dish into a restaurant-quality meal. Remember that the noodles are a blank canvas; season your broth aggressively to ensure every bite is flavorful.
