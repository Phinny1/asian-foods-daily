## **The Ultimate Asian Style Chicken Wings Recipe**

Now, let’s get to the good part—the recipe! I’ve tested this multiple times to ensure it’s foolproof. Follow these steps, and you’ll have finger-licking wings in no time.

### **Ingredients:**

- 2 lbs chicken wings, split into drumettes and flats
- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
- 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar (optional)
- Sesame seeds and chopped green onions for garnish



*Asian Style Chicken Wings*

### **Marinade:**

1. In a large bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, honey, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and rice vinegar.
2. Add the chicken wings to the bowl and toss to coat evenly.
3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (or overnight for maximum flavor).

### **Cooking:**

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or foil for easy cleanup.
3. Arrange the marinated wings in a single layer on the baking sheet.
4. Bake for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the wings are golden and crispy.
5. For extra crispiness, broil for the last 2-3 minutes (watch closely to avoid burning).

### **Serving Suggestions:**

- Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions.
- Serve with a side of steamed rice or a fresh cucumber salad.
- Pair with your favorite dipping sauce, like sriracha mayo or sweet chili sauce.

## **Tips for Perfect Wings Every Time**

- **Pat the wings dry** before marinating to ensure the sauce sticks well.
- **Don’t overcrowd the baking sheet**—give the wings space to crisp up.
- **Use a meat thermometer** to check for doneness (internal temperature should be 165°F).
- **Double the glaze** if you like extra sauce for dipping.
