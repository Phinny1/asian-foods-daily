I once ate this Chinese chicken salad standing over the sink at midnight. The house was quiet, the fridge light my only lamp. Cold chicken, sweet oranges, and a snap of chili-lime clung to crisp greens. Each bite cracked and slipped and sparkled so loudly I thought the neighbors would wake. That’s the sneaky magic of Chinese Chicken Salad: it turns leftovers into a private celebration, no matter the hour.

My grandma called it “clean-out-the-icebox salad.” She’d dump Sunday’s roast, Tuesday’s cabbage, whatever nuts hid in the pantry, and shake it all with soy, honey, and a fistful of ginger. We ate it on the porch step, passing one fork between three grand-kids, racing to catch the last wonton strip before it wilted. I didn’t realize she was teaching me dinner could be fast, bright, and still feel like a hug.

## **Why This Recipe Wins**

Flavor: sweet, salty, sour, and a gentle chili kick in one gulp.  
Texture: soft chicken, crisp cabbage, and three kinds of crunch.  
Ease: chop, toss, eat. Ten [minutes if the chicken](https://asianfoodsdaily.com/easy-orange-chicken-at-home-restaurant-style-in-30-minutes/) waits in the fridge.

## **Ingredients You’ll Need**

- [Cooked chicken](https://asianfoodsdaily.com/how-to-cook-sweet-sour-chicken-thats-addictively-delicious/) – 3 cups, shredded. Rotisserie is fine.
- Napa cabbage – 1 small head, thin sliced. It bends, never tough.
- Romaine – 2 hearts, chopped. Extra crunch and color.
- Carrots – 2 medium, julienned. Sweet pop.
- Mandarin oranges – 1 cup, drained. Little bursts of sunshine.
- Toasted almonds – ½ cup, rough chopped. Nutty backbone.
- Sesame seeds – 2 Tbsp, toasted. Deep, earthy.
- Wonton strips – 1 cup, store-bought. Pure fun.
- Cilantro – a handful, optional but fresh.

Dressing

- 3 Tbsp soy sauce
- 2 Tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 Tbsp lime juice
- 1 Tbsp honey
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp grated ginger
- 1 small garlic clove, smashed
- 1 tsp chili flakes, or less if kids eat

## **How I Make It**

1. Big bowl first. I use the metal one that once held popcorn on movie nights.
2. Pile in cabbage and romaine. Toss with hands. Air between leaves keeps things light.
3. Add carrots, oranges, chicken. No mixing yet. Colors stay brighter that way.
4. Shake dressing in jar. I taste with a lettuce leaf, not a spoon. More lime? Drop it in.
5. Pour dressing over salad. Toss like you mean it. Every strand needs gloss.
6. Shower almonds, sesame, wonton strips on top. One last gentle flip.
7. Serve right away. Bowls, forks, done.

## **Tips That Make a Difference**

- Toast nuts in a dry pan for two minutes. They smell like popcorn and taste twice as nutty.
- Slice cabbage last second. Salt draws water and sog creeps in fast.
- Keep dressing separate if you pack lunch. Pour at noon, shake the box, eat crunchy salad at your desk.
- Swap chicken for shrimp or tofu. Same path, new shoes.

## **Easy Variations**

- Mango cubes instead of mandarins.
- Peanut butter spooned into dressing for a thicker coat.
- Crushed ramen noodles if wonton strips hide from you.
- Add avocado for creamy calm against the chili.

## **Storage and Reheating**

This Chinese Chicken Salad hates the microwave. Store parts solo: greens in one box, chicken in another, dressing in a jar. Combine when hungry. Everything keeps three days. Wonton strips live in a zip bag on the counter or they wilt.

## **Recipe FAQs**

**Can I use bagged slaw mix?**  
Yes. Life is short. Pick the freshest bag.

**How do I make it ahead for a potluck?**  
Layer in a clear bowl: greens, chicken, fruit, nuts. Dress at the table. People ooh and aah when you flip it like a giant salad snow globe.

**Gluten-free?**  
Tamari for soy, rice noodles for crunch. Same happy bowl.

**My kids hate cilantro. Skip it?**  
Sure. Swap in thin-sliced green onion for a mild onion note.

**Can I grill the chicken first?**  
Please do. Smoke adds swagger.

**Can I double the recipe?**  
Triple it. Salad bowls disappear at parties faster than ice in July.

**What if I only have lemon?**  
Use lemon. Salad will still sing, just in a different key.

**Is this Chinese Chicken Salad keto?**  
Drop the oranges and wonton strips. Add extra almonds. Carbs shrink, joy stays.

**Budget tip?**  
Buy yesterday’s rotisserie. Stores mark them down after 7 p.m.

**Vegan version?**  
Tofu cubes and maple syrup instead of honey. Still crave-worthy.

**Can I add noodles?**  
Cook, rinse, chill rice noodles. They tangle nicely.

**Leftover turkey after holidays?**  
Perfect stand-in. Chinese Chicken Salad loves turkey.

**Can I use kale?**  
Massage it first. Kale needs a tender touch.

**Is this salad dinner-worthy?**  
Add a warm baguette. You’ll sleep happy.

**I forgot the sesame oil.**  
A drop of any nut oil rescues the day.

**Can I serve it in wraps?**  
Stuff into tortillas. Crunch travels well.

**Why call it Chinese Chicken Salad?**  
Grandma called it that. Names stick when love is inside.
