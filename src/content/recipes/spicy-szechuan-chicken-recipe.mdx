This Spicy Szechuan Chicken  **(Lázǐ Jī)** recipe delivers the iconic "Mala" (numbing and spicy) flavor profile using crispy fried chicken thighs, toasted Szechuan peppercorns, and a mountain of dried red chilies. It is a high-heat, high-impact dish designed for those seeking restaurant-quality wok cooking at home.

![](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Spicy-Szechuan-Chicken-1536.webp)

## What Is Spicy Szechuan Chicken?

Spicy Szechuan Chicken, known in China as *Lázǐ Jī* (辣子鸡), is a cornerstone of Szechuan cuisine originating from the Geleshan district of Chongqing. Unlike Westernized versions, the authentic dish features small, crispy nuggets of chicken buried in a vibrant sea of dried chilies and Szechuan peppercorns. The goal is "numbing heat"—a sensory experience where the peppercorns vibrate on the tongue (the *Ma* effect) while the chilies provide the fire (the *La* effect).

## Why This Recipe Works

- **Double-Fry Technique:** Ensures the chicken remains crispy even after being tossed with aromatics.
- **Aromatic Infusion:** By toasting the peppercorns and chilies in oil first, we create a flavored base that permeates the meat.
- **Balanced Acidity:** The use of Shaoxing wine and a touch of sugar cuts through the intense heat and oil.
- **Velveting:** A light cornstarch marinade keeps the chicken juicy inside despite the high-heat frying.

## Ingredients You’ll Need For Spicy Szechuan Chicken

![](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Spicy-Szechuan-Chicken-2-1536.webp)

**The Protein**

- 1.5 lbs Boneless, skinless chicken thighs (cut into 1-inch cubes)
- 2 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- 1 tbsp Light soy sauce
- 1/2 tsp White pepper
- 3 tbsp Cornstarch

**The Spice & Aromatics**

- 2 cups Dried Szechuan red chilies (snipped in half, seeds removed for less heat)
- 2 tbsp Whole Szechuan peppercorns
- 3 tbsp Neutral oil (Grapeseed or Peanut)
- 5 cloves Garlic (thinly sliced)
- 2 inches Ginger (peeled and julienned)
- 3 Green onions (whites and greens separated, cut into 1-inch lengths)

**The Seasoning**

- 1 tsp Toasted sesame oil
- 1 tsp Sugar
- 1 tbsp Toasted sesame seeds

## Equipment

- **Carbon Steel Wok:** Essential for achieving *Wok Hei* (breath of the wok).
- **Spider Strainer:** For safe removal of chicken from hot oil.
- **Instant-Read Thermometer:** To monitor oil temperature (target 350°F/175°C).

## How to Make  Spicy Szechuan Chicken (Step-by-Step)

![](https://cdn.pagesmith.app/97394d4a/images/Spicy-Szechuan-Chicken-4-1536.webp)

1. **Marinate:** Combine chicken, Shaoxing wine, soy sauce, white pepper, and cornstarch. Let sit for 20 minutes.
2. **First Fry:** Heat 2 inches of oil in the wok to 350°F. Fry chicken in batches until pale gold (about 3–4 minutes). Remove and drain.
3. **Second Fry:** Increase heat until oil reaches 375°F. Flash-fry the chicken for 1 minute until deep golden and crispy. Remove and set aside.
4. **Aromatic Base:** Drain most oil, leaving 2 tablespoons. Over medium-low heat, add Szechuan peppercorns and dried chilies. Stir-fry until fragrant and chilies turn slightly darker (do not burn).
5. **The Sauté:** Add ginger, garlic, and the white parts of the green onions. Increase heat to high.
6. **The Combine:** Return the chicken to the wok. Toss rapidly for 60 seconds to coat the chicken in the infused oil.
7. **Finish:** Stir in sugar, sesame oil, sesame seeds, and the remaining green onion tops. Serve immediately.

## Pro Tips For Perfect **Lázǐ Jī**

- **Peppercorn Quality:** Use fresh, bright red Szechuan peppercorns. If they are brown or dusty, the numbing effect will be negligible.
- **Chili Management:** You are not meant to eat all the chilies. They are there to flavor the oil and the chicken.
- **Dry the Chicken:** Ensure the chicken is patted dry before marinading to help the cornstarch adhere.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Burning the Chilies:** Dried chilies burn in seconds. Keep them moving and adjust heat if they turn black.
- **Overcrowding the Wok:** Frying too much chicken at once drops the oil temperature, leading to soggy, greasy meat.
- **Skipping the Second Fry:** The first fry cooks the meat; the second fry creates the structural crunch.

## Easy Variations

- **Szechuan Tofu:** Replace chicken with extra-firm pressed tofu cubes.
- **Vegetable Boost:** Add diced bell peppers or celery for additional texture.
- **Mild Version:** Use dried Guajillo chilies or Korean Gochugaru for color without the extreme Scoville heat.

## Serving Suggestions

- **Grains:** Serve with steamed Jasmine rice to absorb the spicy oil.
- **Side Dish:** A cold smashed cucumber salad (Pai Huang Gua) provides a necessary cooling contrast.
- **Beverage:** A crisp Lager or cold Chrysanthemum tea helps mitigate the "Ma La" sensation.

## Storage and Reheating

- **Storage:** Keep in an airtight container for up to 3 days.
- **Reheating:** Do not microwave (it will make the chicken rubbery). Reheat in an air fryer at 350°F for 4–5 minutes or in a dry skillet over medium heat until crispy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use chicken breast?** A: Yes, but thighs are preferred for their higher fat content, which prevents drying out during the double-fry process.

**Q: What is a substitute for Shaoxing wine?** A: Dry pale sherry is the closest substitute. In a pinch, use Mirin (though reduce the added sugar in the recipe).

**Q: Why isn't my tongue tingling?** A: Your Szechuan peppercorns may be old. The numbing chemical, *hydroxy-alpha-sanshool*, degrades over time.

## Final Note

Mastering Spicy Szechuan Chicken is about controlling the heat of the oil and the timing of the aromatics. Once you achieve the perfect balance of crispy texture and numbing spice, this dish becomes a recurring staple for any serious home cook.
