## **What You’ll Need: Ingredients**

Before we start , let’s gather everything. I’ve kept this list simple, with substitutes for anything tricky to find.

### **For the Marinade:**

- **Chicken**: 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs (thighs stay juicier, but breasts work too)
- **Yogurt**: ½ cup plain Greek yogurt (it makes the marinade thick and clingy)
- **Lemon Juice**: 1 tablespoon (to tenderize the meat)
- **Ginger-Garlic Paste**: 1 tablespoon (or 1 tsp each minced ginger and garlic)
- **Spices**:
  - 1 tsp garam masala (adds that warm, cozy flavor)
  - 1 tsp turmeric (for a golden hue)
  - 1 tsp chili powder (adjust for your spice tolerance)
  - Salt to taste

### **For the Sauce:**

- **Butter**: 3 tablespoons (unsalted gives you control over saltiness)
- **Onion**: 1 large, finely chopped
- **Ginger-Garlic Paste**: 1 tablespoon
- **Tomatoes**: 2 large, pureed (or 1 cup canned tomato puree for convenience)
- **Spices**:
  - 1 tsp cumin seeds (for a nutty kick)
  - 1 tsp garam masala
  - 1 tsp coriander powder
  - ½ tsp red chili powder (optional, for heat lovers)
- **Kasuri Methi**: 1 tablespoon dried fenugreek leaves (the secret to that authentic taste; sub with a pinch of fenugreek seeds if needed)
- **Cream**: ½ cup heavy cream (or coconut cream for dairy-free)
- **Sugar**: 1 teaspoon (to balance tomato acidity)
- **Salt** to taste
- **Fresh Cilantro**: chopped, for garnish

## **Step-by-Step: Crafting Your Butter Chicken**

Let’s break this down into manageable steps. Trust me, it’s easier than it looks!

### **Step 1: Marinate the Chicken**

The marinade is where the flavor starts, so don’t skip it:

- Cut your chicken into bite-sized pieces.
- In a bowl, mix yogurt, lemon juice, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, turmeric, chili powder, and salt.
- Toss in the chicken, coat it well, and let it chill in the fridge for at least 1 hour (overnight if you can—it’s worth it!).

**Why It Matters**: This step tenderizes the chicken and infuses it with spice. It’s the foundation of that melt-in-your-mouth texture.

### **Step 2: Cook the Chicken**

No tandoor? No problem! Here are two simple ways to cook it:

- **Oven**: Preheat to 400°F (200°C). Spread the chicken on a baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes until cooked through.
- **Stovetop**: Heat a bit of oil in a pan over medium heat. Cook the chicken 5-7 minutes per side until golden.

**Watch Out**: Don’t overcook! Cut a piece to check—it should be white inside, no pink. Overdone chicken gets tough, and we want juicy.

### **Step 3: Whip Up the Creamy Tomato Sauce**

While the chicken cooks, let’s make that luscious sauce:

- Melt butter in a large pan over medium heat.
- Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for 30 seconds.
- Toss in the chopped onion and cook until golden (5-7 minutes).
- Stir in ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
- Add tomato puree and cook for 5 minutes, stirring now and then.
- Mix in garam masala, coriander powder, chili powder (if using), and salt. Cook 2-3 minutes more.
- Crush kasuri methi in your hands and sprinkle it in—it’s the game-changer!

**Pro Tip**: Want a silky sauce? Blend it with an immersion blender before adding the chicken.

### **Step 4: Combine and Simmer**

Here’s where it all comes together:

- Add the cooked chicken to the sauce and simmer for 10 minutes.
- Stir in cream and sugar, then taste—adjust salt or spices as needed.
- Simmer 5 more minutes until the sauce is thick and creamy.

**Heart Moment**: That first whiff of buttery, spiced goodness? I live for it. Sneak a taste—you’ve earned it!

### **Step 5: Garnish and Serve**

- Sprinkle fresh cilantro on top for color and a fresh pop.
- Serve hot with naan, rice, or roti. Heaven on a plate!

**Bonus Idea**: Whip up garlic naan—brush naan with melted butter and minced garlic, then toast in a pan.

## **Top Tips for Butter Chicken Perfection**

- **Chicken Choice**: Thighs over breasts for juiciness.
- **Marinate Longer**: More time = more flavor.
- **Flavor Balance**: Too tangy? Add sugar. Too rich? A squeeze of lemon.
- **Meal Prep**: It’s even better the next day—perfect for leftovers!
