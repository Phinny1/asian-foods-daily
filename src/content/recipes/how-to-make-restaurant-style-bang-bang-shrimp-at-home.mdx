## **What Is Bang Bang Shrimp?**

Bang Bang Shrimp is the kind of appetizer that steals the show. Picture this: plump shrimp, fried to a perfect crisp, then tossed in a sauce that’s creamy, sweet, and spicy all at once. The magic lies in the combo—mayonnaise, sweet chili sauce, and a generous hit of sriracha. It’s often sprinkled with green onions or cilantro and served over lettuce or with rice. The name “Bang Bang” isn’t just catchy; it’s a nod to the explosive flavors that hit your taste buds with every bite. Trust me, this isn’t your average shrimp dish.

## **How to Make Bang Bang Shrimp at Home**

Ready to play chef? You don’t need a culinary degree to nail this. Here’s a foolproof recipe to [bring Bang Bang Shrimp to your table](https://asianfoodsdaily.com/the-beef-kaldereta-that-brings-everyone-to-the-table/).

### **Ingredients:**

- 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 1/2 cup cornstarch
- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 1/4 cup sweet chili sauce
- 1-2 tbsp sriracha (dial it up or down to your taste)
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp onion powder
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Vegetable oil for frying
- Green onions or cilantro for garnish
- Lettuce or rice for serving

### **Instructions:**

1. **Prep the Shrimp:** Pat those shrimp dry with paper towels—soggy shrimp won’t crisp up. Toss them in a bowl with cornstarch, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper until they’re nicely coated.
2. **Fry Time:** Heat about an inch of veggie oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. When it’s shimmering, fry the shrimp in batches—2-3 minutes per side until they’re golden and crunchy. Drain them on a paper towel-lined plate.
3. **Sauce It Up:** In a bowl, whisk together mayo, sweet chili sauce, sriracha, and honey. Taste it—too tame? Add more sriracha. Too spicy? Ease up next time.
4. **Toss and Serve:** Coat the fried shrimp in that glorious sauce. Serve it hot over lettuce or rice, and sprinkle some green onions or cilantro on top for flair.

## **Tips for Perfect Bang Bang Shrimp**

- **Don’t Overcook:** Shrimp turn rubbery fast. Watch them like a hawk while frying.
- **Spice Control:** Start light with sriracha if you’re spice-shy, or go wild if you’re a heat fiend.
- **Prep Ahead:** Mix the sauce early and chill it—just don’t toss it with the shrimp until they’re fresh from the fryer.
- **Bake Option:** Want less oil? Bake the shrimp at 400°F for 10-12 minutes. They won’t be as crispy, but still tasty.
